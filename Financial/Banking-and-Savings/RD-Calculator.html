How is RD interest calculated?
RD uses quarterly compound interest but paid only at maturity. Formula: M = P Ã— [(1+i)^n - 1] / [1 - (1+i)^(-1/3)], where P=monthly deposit, i=quarterly interest rate, n=quarters. Example: â‚¹5,000/month for 5 years at 7.5% = Maturity â‚¹3,59,854 (Invested â‚¹3L, Interest â‚¹59,854). Unlike FD where full amount earns interest from day 1, RD calculates on each monthly installment separately. First installment earns interest for full tenure, last installment for just 1 month. That's why RD interest is lower than FD for same rate and tenure.
What happens if I miss RD installment?
Banks charge â‚¹1-5 per â‚¹100 monthly deposit as penalty for missed installments. Example: â‚¹5,000 RD missed attracts â‚¹50-250 penalty. If irregular income, choose lower monthly amount you can sustain. Can increase RD amount later by opening additional RD. Penalty reduces maturity returns significantly.
RD vs SIP - Which is better?
RD: Fixed returns (7-8%), capital safe, govt insured up to â‚¹5L, good for risk-averse/short-term (1-3 years). SIP: Variable returns (10-15% historically), market-linked risk, good for long-term (5+ years), wealth creation, tax-free after 1 year (LTCG â‚¹1.25L exempt). Comparison: â‚¹5,000 monthly for 10 years - RD @7.5% = â‚¹8.73L, SIP @12% = â‚¹11.61L (â‚¹2.88L extra!). Strategy: Use RD for emergency fund/short goals, SIP for retirement/long-term wealth. Start both - 70% SIP, 30% RD for balanced risk-return. RD acts as stable base, SIP builds corpus.
Can I break RD before maturity?
Yes, but with 1-2% interest penalty on earned interest. Some banks allow premature closure only after minimum 3-6 months. Penalty varies: SBI charges 1% on interest, HDFC 1-2%, PNB 2%. In emergency, better to break RD than take personal loan at 15-24%! Partial withdrawal not allowed (unlike some large FDs). Consider: If you need regular access, keep liquid savings in high-interest savings account (4-7%) instead of RD. Or use RD sweep-in facility where excess funds auto-transfer to RD but available when needed. Systematic planning avoids premature closure penalty.

Is there TDS on RD interest?
Yes, 10% TDS if total interest across all FDs+RDs in a bank exceeds â‚¹40,000/year (â‚¹50,000 for senior citizens). However, RD interest accrues quarterly but paid only at maturity, so TDS deducted only when maturity amount is paid, not annually like FD. Submit Form 15G (under 60) or 15H (senior citizens) if total annual income below taxable limit to avoid TDS. RD interest is added to income and taxed at your slab rate (0%, 5%, 20%, 30%). For 30% bracket: Effective return on 7.5% RD = only 5.25% post-tax. Consider tax-free PPF (7.1%) or equity ELSS SIP for better post-tax returns if you're in higher tax brackets.
