How is FD interest calculated and compounded?
FD interest is calculated using compound interest formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where P=principal, r=rate, n=compounding frequency, t=time. Quarterly compounding (most common) means interest is added 4 times yearly. Example: â‚¹1 lakh at 7.5% for 5 years quarterly compounded = â‚¹1,45,388 maturity vs â‚¹1,43,750 with annual compounding - saving â‚¹1,638 extra. Monthly compounding gives slightly better returns than quarterly.
What is TDS on FD and how to avoid it?
Banks deduct 10% TDS if FD interest exceeds â‚¹40,000/year (â‚¹50,000 for senior citizens). Submit Form 15G (below 60 years) or 15H (above 60) if total income is below taxable limit to avoid TDS. Interest is added to income and taxed at your slab rate. For 30% tax bracket, effective return on 7.5% FD is only 5.25%. Consider tax-free options like PPF (7.1%) or equity investments for better post-tax returns if you're in higher tax brackets.
Can I break FD before maturity and what are charges?
Yes, premature withdrawal is allowed but banks charge penalty of 0.5-1% on interest rate. Example: 7% FD withdrawn early gets only 6-6.5%. Some banks offer partial withdrawal from large FDs. Alternative: Sweep-in/Flexi FD links to savings account, auto-transfers excess funds to FD, and breaks only required amount during need - avoiding full FD premature penalty. Consider FD laddering: divide amount in 1,2,3,5 year FDs so some mature regularly for liquidity without breaking others.

What is FD laddering strategy and its benefits?
FD laddering means splitting money across multiple FDs with staggered maturity dates instead of one large FD. Example: â‚¹5 lakh divided into â‚¹1 lakh each for 1,2,3,4,5 years. Benefits: (1) Liquidity - one FD matures yearly, (2) Rate flexibility - reinvest matured FD at current best rates, (3) Risk mitigation - not locked at single rate if rates increase, (4) Emergency access without breaking all FDs. Ideal for retirees needing regular income or uncertain rate environments. Reinvest matured amount for longest tenure to maintain ladder.
Should I choose cumulative or non-cumulative FD?
Cumulative FD: Interest compounds and paid at maturity - best for wealth building. Non-cumulative: Interest paid monthly/quarterly/yearly - ideal for regular income needs like retirees. Example: â‚¹10 lakh at 7.5% for 5 years - Cumulative gives â‚¹14.54 lakh (â‚¹4.54L interest), Non-cumulative monthly payout gives â‚¹6,250/month totaling â‚¹3.75L interest. Cumulative returns 21% more due to compounding. Choose cumulative if you don't need immediate income. For senior citizens needing monthly income, non-cumulative quarterly/monthly payout with senior rate (8%) provides steady cash flow.
